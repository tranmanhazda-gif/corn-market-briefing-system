# Corn Market Summary

## July 23, 2026

### Today's Summary
Corn has been in a clear uptrend since the end of June, rising from around 420 to over 480 on the nearest contract. The 20-day moving average has climbed steadily, the 50-day dipped briefly before turning back up, and the 100-day is drifting up slightly - overall the moving averages are starting to stack in a bullish order after a sharp late-June selloff that the market now looks to be recovering from.

### Bullish Factors
- Price trend: higher highs and higher lows since late June, textbook uptrend
- July WASDE cut 2026/27 ending stocks by 170 million bushels versus June's estimate, tightening the supply picture
- Production was actually raised slightly this month, so the stocks cut looks like it's coming from stronger demand/use (feed, exports, or ethanol) rather than a weaker crop - tighter available supply either way

### Bearish Factors
- Trading volume in July looks noticeably thinner than earlier peaks this year (Jan, May) - the rally may not have strong participation behind it yet
- The recent ~5% single-day move sits out near the edge of this market's normal range (most days cluster near 0%, with the full year's range only about -6% to +6%) - a move that size deserves extra skepticism until it's confirmed by follow-through
- 2026/27 production was actually raised slightly this month, which is a mildly offsetting bearish fact underneath the bullish stocks headline - the tightening story isn't as clean as it looks at first glance

### Market Outlook
Leaning bullish over the next 1-4 weeks given the trend and the tighter stocks picture, but I'd want to see volume pick back up to confirm the move has real conviction before getting more confident. Watching the August 12 WASDE report closely, since that's the next major catalyst.

### Confidence Score
7/10 - I trust the trend and the fundamentals story, but the thin volume genuinely bothers me enough to not go higher than this.
